About Me

I work as a Data Engineer and Architect, helping teams build systems that solve real problems—whether that's designing cloud data platforms, improving pipelines, or making data easier to access and use.

Over the years, I’ve worked across different levels of the organization, from hands-on development to architectural planning. I focus on building solutions that are reliable, scalable, and aligned with business goals—while keeping things clear and collaborative along the way.

I’ve found that strong communication and teamwork go a long way when navigating complex projects. They’re what keep things moving and make great outcomes possible. Below are a few examples of my work. You can find more details on my LinkedIn.

The Data Observability Toolkit (DOT), developed by DataKind, is an open-source solution that helps organizations monitor and improve the quality of their data pipelines. It supports transparency, accuracy, and reliability by enabling error tracking, data flow monitoring, and better decision-making. While DOT offers powerful capabilities, its installation process can be complex—especially for users unfamiliar with tools like Docker, Apache Airflow, PostgreSQL, and AppSmith.
